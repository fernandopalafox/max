## Pretraining Runs — TDMPC2 / MAML / FOMAML

### What we did

Investigated an apparent TDMPC2 training regression — determined it was a GPU difference (RTX 4090 → RTX A4000 at 140W power cap, ~86 sps vs ~164 sps), no code regression.

Updated all cheetah configs to match TDMPC2 paper hyperparameters: MPPI 512 samples / 6 iterations, `min_buffer_size=2500`, `cheetah_mass_scale=1.0`, `chunk_size=1000`, `eval_freq=10000`, `num_episodes=2`.

Added `meta_tdmpc2` and `fomaml_tdmpc2` trainers to `max/trainers.py`. Both use an inner loop that simulates one gradient step on the dynamics adapter before computing the outer TDMPC2 loss. `meta_lr_inner=4e-5` matches the winning OGD LR for LoRA-XS adaptation from the Apr 14 experiments. FOMAML drops second-order terms via a straight-through estimator.

Launched 1M step pretraining runs for TDMPC2 (dense), MAML (LoRA-XS), and FOMAML (LoRA-XS) from scratch. Checkpoints saved every 100k steps.

### LoRA-XS unfrozen backbone: SVD subspace bug and fix

**Problem:** The original LoRA-XS used static SVD factors (U, S, V) computed once from W at init and baked into the Python closure. With a trainable backbone (`frozen_backbone=false`), W drifts during training but U/S/V stay fixed — the adapter R operates in an increasingly misaligned subspace. On checkpoint reload, U/S/V are recomputed from the saved W, so the subspace changes entirely and any meta-learned R init is invalidated.

**Fix:** Replaced static SVD with trainable projection matrices P and Q. New parametrization: `W_eff = W + P @ R @ Q^T`. P and Q co-adapt with W in the outer loop; only R is adapted in the inner loop and at deployment. No SVD, no stale subspace, no checkpoint mismatch. P, Q, and R are all stored flat under `params["mean"]["dynamics"]["adapter"]` and saved automatically with every checkpoint.

New config flag `freeze_subspace`: `false` during pretraining (P, Q trainable), `true` at deployment (P, Q baked into closure, only R adapted online).
